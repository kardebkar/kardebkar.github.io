Research focus

Core problem

Sensing pipelines fail in practice due to domain shift, sensor noise, missingness, latency constraints, and weak supervision. I work at the intersection of ML and systems to build methods and tools that stay reliable when sensors and environments drift.
  • Robust sensing ML: generalization under shift, anomaly detection, uncertainty.
  • Time-series intelligence: streaming inference, stabilization, and evaluation.
  • AI systems: reproducible experiments, deployment workflows, and observability.

Methods

Deep learning, probabilistic modeling, and systems engineering for end-to-end prototypes.

Projects

Agentic Hyperspectral Analyst (AHA)

Multi-agent workflow for autonomous hyperspectral analysis: anomaly detection, adaptive path planning, and diagnosis.
Implements a sense-model-plan-act loop with a 3D convolutional β-VAE (anomaly scoring), dueling DQN (investigation policies), and conditional GANs (diagnosis). Designed with explicit security/audit considerations and human-in-the-loop gates.
PythonVAEDQNcGANhyperspectral

Wrist2Avatar: Apple Watch IMU Streaming + Activity Recognition

Streaming wearable IMU pipeline with labeling, baseline training, and real-time classification + stabilization.
Streams accelerometer/gyroscope data from Apple Watch to macOS via iPhone relay, logs labeled CSV, trains a baseline model, and runs real-time inference with state smoothing for downstream control (e.g., avatars/adaptive UX).
